Frequently Asked Questions

As leading wholesale coffee roasters, you can be sure that you will receive the freshest coffee possible from our roastery at The Coffee Officina. We will send our orders no later than 48 hours after you have placed your order with us. We send all our orders with Royal Mail and will use a courier if the order is over 2kg.

Unfortunately due to the increasing complexity and Customs and Duty charge of crossing borders at the moment, we now only offer delivery to the United Kingdom until further notice. However, if you still would like to go ahead with your order, please get in touch and we will happily take your order and quote for shipping if relevant. To find out more about Customs charges and taxation policies, please contact your local customs office or visit their official website. If you would like more information about our delivery options, please find our delivery policy, here.

As coffee is perishable, we unfortunately don’t accept returns on roasted coffee. However, if there are any errors with your order or your coffee, contact us within 2 days of receiving your order and we will do our best to help. If you wish to return brewing equipment, accessories or magazines, you will need to do so within 30 days of receiving your order. Unfortunately, we are unable to refund or exchange any product after 30 days. Please note that your item must be in the same condition as when you received it, unused and in the original packaging.

At The Coffee Officina, we want to help businesses to grow and a barista training course can help your café or restaurant to get ahead of your competitors. Therefore, we offer a variety of different levels of training, as well as coffee knowledge classes. These are held at either our roastery or your premises. For more information and to book our classes or courses, get in touch with our helpful team.

Yes! We have a wide range of coffee equipment for you to choose from either to use at home to enhance your coffee experience or for your café/restaurant. We stock the leading brands when it comes to our equipment, including Sanremo, Iberital and Fiorenzato. From espresso machines to coffee grinders, you can find the equipment that best suits your requirements in our range. All our equipment is top of the range and leading in technology for a seamless process.

As well as providing our customers with delicious and flavoursome coffee and useful equipment, we also help customers and business to learn more about coffee. We have outlined the harvesting and processing of our coffee beans from the different types of picking right through to the pulp natural process, so you can have a better understanding of how your coffee got to your mug. If you would like more information about harvesting and processing than what is already on our site, get in touch and we would be more than happy to tell you more!

If you would like to learn how to brew coffee in the comfort of your own home, you have come to the right place! Regardless of the style you want to try or the equipment you have available, we have a number of brewing methods to follow to produce the perfect cup of coffee. From pour over to using a French press, a moka pot and even an AeroPress, you can find the easy to follow steps on our brewing methods page or you can buy them here .

Yes! We roast our coffee at our roastery in Matching Tye, Essex. At The Coffee Officina, roasting is a vital part of the process to help to showcase the unique flavours that are locked inside the coffee. We believe that every type of coffee should be treated differently during the roasting process, so when we hand roast our coffee, we adjust the roast profile accordingly. You can find out more by reading ‘our story’, or get in touch with our team, today.
